# UAE Tokenized Real Estate Regulation: Arabic-Source Intelligence Report

**Prepared for:** Antweave (subsidiary of Sky Blue Group) — VARA Broker-Dealer licensing pathway for tokenized real estate distribution
**Research date:** 17 April 2026
**Methodology:** Arabic-first searches across UAE government portals and Arabic regional press, with English verification; original Arabic terminology preserved in parentheses for bilingual RAG indexing.

---

## Executive summary: what the Arabic record tells us

Dubai has built **the world's first end-to-end real estate tokenization framework** and operationalized it in live retail production by mid-2025. The Arabic-language evidence — particularly the Dubai Land Department Arabic tokenization portal (dubailand.gov.ae/ar), Dubai Media Office Arabic releases (mediaoffice.ae/ar), Law No. 4 of 2022 Arabic text, and VARA's Arabic news feed (vara.ae/ar) — reveals three facts that English-only research materially understates: (i) the DLD pilot is **deliberately narrow**, admitting only two named firms (Prypco, Ctrl Alt) with an explicit Arabic-language "open door" for additional VARA-licensed firms; (ii) DLD itself (not VARA alone) **reviews property pricing fairness** before any listing — a gating step barely mentioned in English coverage; and (iii) as of **1 January 2026**, the Securities & Commodities Authority (SCA / هيئة الأوراق المالية والسلع) was **replaced by the Capital Market Authority** (CMA / هيئة سوق المال) under Federal Decree-Laws 32/2025 and 33/2025, introducing **extraterritorial reach over any person targeting UAE clients** — directly relevant to Antweave's Luxembourg SPV structure.

For Antweave specifically: the DMCC entity is correctly positioned within VARA's jurisdiction (Law 4/2022 Article 3 excludes only DIFC); a VARA Broker-Dealer licence is achievable on 6–12 month timelines; Ctrl Alt (VL/25/05/002) is the incumbent DLD partner while Tokinvest (VL/2024/12/004) offers a more open multi-asset marketplace route; and a Luxembourg SPV + ERC-3643 structure is workable but requires (a) separate VARA Category 1 issuance approval, (b) CMA product-level registration, and (c) no AED-pegged coupon issuance (CBUAE's exclusive remit under the Payment Token Services Regulation).

## 1. VARA (هيئة تنظيم الأصول الافتراضية) — Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority

### 1.1 Legal foundation

Dubai **Law No. 4 of 2022** (القانون رقم 4 لسنة 2022 بشأن تنظيم الأصول الافتراضية في إمارة دبي) established VARA as the sole authority regulating virtual assets across all of Dubai. Article 3 Arabic text states: **"تُطبّق أحكام هذا القانون على خدمات الأصول الافتراضيّة التي يتم تقديمها في كافّة أنحاء الإمارة، بما في ذلك مناطق التطوير الخاصّة والمناطق الحُرّة، باستثناء مركز دبي المالي العالمي"** ("The provisions of this law apply to virtual asset services provided throughout the Emirate, including special development zones and free zones, **except for the Dubai International Financial Centre**"). Source: dlp.dubai.gov.ae [Arabic]. **This places DMCC squarely within VARA's jurisdiction** — a foundational point for Antweave's existing DMCC entity.

Federal complements identified only via Arabic-language government portals:
- **Cabinet Decision No. 111/2022** (قرار مجلس الوزراء 111 لسنة 2022) — federal virtual asset oversight framework [u.ae/ar; aletihad.ae]
- **Cabinet Decision No. 99/2024** — administrative penalties schedule for VA violations
- **Cabinet Decision No. 83/2025** — VASP service fees

### 1.2 Rulebook architecture (Virtual Assets and Related Activities Regulations 2023)

VARA's rulebook, effective 7 February 2023 and materially amended 19 June 2025, comprises four **mandatory compulsory rulebooks** (Company, Compliance & Risk Management, Technology & Information, Market Conduct) plus seven **activity-specific rulebooks** and a **Virtual Asset Issuance Rulebook** (دليل قواعد إصدار الأصول الافتراضية). Source: rulebooks.vara.ae [English — Arabic news/notices exist but full technical rulebooks are English-only official]. **Arabic-source finding:** VARA's Arabic news portal (vara.ae/ar/news) hosts press releases, enforcement notices, and National Risk Assessment AML/CFT circulars — but the binding rulebook text is published only in English. This is material: Arabic-speaking counsel must rely on English technical documents.

### 1.3 Broker-Dealer Services Rulebook (current version 19 June 2025)

Four sub-parts: Part I Policies & Disclosures; Part II Trading & Execution Rules; Part III Margin Trading; **Part IV Licensed Distribution Services** (newly added). Permitted activities include order arranging, solicitation and acceptance of orders and fiat, matching, principal trading, market-making, and — critically for Antweave — **placement, distribution and other issuance-related services to clients issuing Virtual Assets**. [Source: rulebooks.vara.ae, English]

### 1.4 Issuance framework — three categories (October 2025 guidance; April 2026 refinements)

| Category | Description | Antweave relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Category 1 (الفئة 1)** | Licensed issuance — includes Fiat-Referenced VAs (FRVA) and **Asset-Referenced VAs (ARVA / أصل افتراضي مرجعي)** | **Real estate tokens = ARVA** |
| **Category 2 (الفئة 2)** | Distribution facilitated via Licensed Distributor conducting issuer due diligence | Broker-Dealer route for third-party issuers |
| **Exempt** | Non-transferable or closed-loop redeemable tokens | Not applicable |

Dubai became **"the first jurisdiction in the world to establish dedicated guidance for virtual asset issuance"** per Arabic Zawya coverage [Arabic: zawya.com/ar: "دبي تصبح أول سلطة قضائية..."].

### 1.5 ARVA (real estate) issuer requirements

Sourced from the VARA Issuance Rulebook Annex 2 as described in Arabic regulatory commentary and cross-verified to English primary text:
- **Paid-up capital:** the higher of **AED 1,500,000** or **2% of the 24-month average market value of reserve assets**
- Legally segregated reserves from issuer assets, audited and monthly-disclosed (token count, circulating value, reserve composition, backing status)
- **Redemption right** in AED or equivalent means specified in the whitepaper, **executed without fee within a reasonable timeframe**
- **Semi-annual audits** of circulating assets and reserves + annual financial audit with senior management certification
- **Whitepaper and Risk Disclosure Statement** mandatory for all non-exempt issuers
- **Use of "stablecoin"** prohibited except where specific criteria are met

[Arabic commentary: cryptoverselawyers.io, panewslab; English primary: rulebooks.vara.ae]

### 1.6 Licence fees (VARA Schedule 2)

One-time application fee **AED 100,000** per activity (Broker-Dealer, Custody, Exchange, Lending-Borrowing, Management & Investment); **AED 40,000** for Advisory and Transfer & Settlement. Annual supervision fee **AED 200,000/year** for the former, **AED 80,000** for Transfer & Settlement. Licence modification **AED 500**; withdrawal **AED 10,000**. 50% of fees due at application, balance at approval. [Sources: 10leaves.ae, ape.law]

### 1.7 Marketing Regulations (لوائح التسويق) — effective 1 October 2024

Apply to **all entities whether VARA-licensed or not**, and to marketing **in or targeting the UAE** (not just Dubai). Arabic guidance published 26 September 2024 with 90-day grace period. Key prohibitions:
- Marketing of VA services/assets in the UAE is forbidden unless the entity is VARA-licensed or acts on behalf of one with approval
- Mandatory risk warnings; prohibition of guaranteed-return claims
- Influencer and agency accountability
- **Record retention minimum 8 years**
- **Penalties up to AED 10 million per violation**

[Sources: rulebooks.vara.ae, Linklaters, CMS; Arabic summaries on neoslegal.co]

## 5. ADGM FSRA (هيئة تنظيم الخدمات المالية – سوق أبوظبي العالمي)

### 5.1 Classification taxonomy

ADGM applies a substance test under Financial Services and Markets Regulations 2015 (FSMR):
- **Digital Securities (الأوراق المالية الرقمية)** — tokens with security characteristics (shares, debentures, fund units); regulated identically to traditional securities under FSP authorisation
- **Virtual Assets (الأصول الافتراضية)** — treated as **commodities**; only "Accepted Virtual Assets" (AVAs) list may be used
- **Fiat-Referenced Tokens / FRTs (العملات الرقمية الثابتة المدعومة بالعملات الورقية)** — added as distinct regulated activity in **December 2024** post Consultation Paper 7/2024

**[Arabic source]** Argaam: **"يجعل الإطار إصدار العملات الرقمية الثابتة المدعومة بالعملات الورقية (FRTs) نشاطًا منظمًا مميزًا ضمن النظام الشامل لتنظيم الخدمات المالية لدى أبوظبي العالمي"** ("The framework makes FRT issuance a distinct regulated activity within ADGM's comprehensive financial services regulatory system"). [argaam.com]

**Tokenized real-estate classification in ADGM:** fractional-property tokens conveying rights to rental income or SPV equity are treated as **Digital Securities**, triggering FSMR issuance, trading (MTF), custody and arranger permissions — not the VA regime.

### 5.2 Licensed activities & capital requirements (post-June 2025 amendments)

FSRA issued **120 in-principle approvals in 2025 (+32% YoY)** and 94 new FSP permissions per Zawya Arabic. [zawya.com/ar]
- Base minimum capital ~**USD 250,000** for most digital-asset categories
- Category 4 uplifted from USD 10,000 to **USD 50,000** in 2025
- MTF regulatory capital = 6 months' opex + 6 months' buffer
- **Privacy coins and algorithmic stablecoins prohibited**
- Venture Capital Funds permitted to invest in VAs

### 5.3 Named ADGM-licensed firms (digital assets)

Over 20 regulated firms as of late 2025. Named:
- **Binance** — ADGM global FSRA licence December 2025 (first crypto exchange with worldwide ADGM licence) [zawya.com/ar, Arabic]
- **Circle** — ADGM FSP
- **GFO-X** — IPA for digital-asset derivatives MTF/clearing, 2026 launch
- **VersiFi** — IPA for DMA and OTC digital-asset trading
- **Hayvn (هيفن)** — FSP arranging + VA custody [albayan.ae, Arabic]

**Real estate–specific:** Mantra × DAMAC (**USD 1bn**) and Mantra × MAG Keturah (**USD 500m**) are structured tokenization transactions involving ADGM ecosystem but not necessarily ADGM licences held by Mantra itself. [coindesk.com]

### 5.4 DLT Foundations Regulations 2023

World-first regime for ownerless blockchain foundations. Council of ≥2 Councillors; optional Guardian; mandatory Charter; Tokenomics Paper for token-issuing foundations. Useful as an SPV wrapper for tokenized real estate governance, though an FSP licence is still required for regulated financial activities. [adgm.com]

## 6. DFSA (هيئة دبي للخدمات المالية) — DIFC

### 6.1 Investment Token Regime (October 2021+)

**Investment Token (رمز استثمار)** = token with characteristics of a Security or Derivative. Tokens representing fractional property ownership or SPV units = Investment Tokens. Amended 2024 for AML, consumer protection, technology governance.

Al Tamimi Arabic: **"تصنيف الرمز العقاري – رمز منفعة أو رمز دفع أو رمزًا شبيهًا بالأوراق المالية – هو الذي يُحدّد الالتزامات المتعلقة بالترخيص والإفصاح والحفظ الأمين والتسويق"** ("Classification of the real-estate token — utility, payment, or security-like — determines obligations of licensing, disclosure, safe custody and marketing"). [tamimi.com, Arabic]

### 6.2 Tokenisation Regulatory Sandbox — launched March 2025

Under the **Innovation Testing Licence (ITL)** extension. Received **96 expressions of interest** globally. Covers issuance, trading, holding and settlement of Investment Tokens including real estate, bonds, sukuk, equities. [news.uppersetup.com/ar, Arabic]

### 6.3 Crypto Token Regime reform — effective 12 January 2026

DFSA **abolished the Recognised Crypto Tokens (RCT) list** for non-fiat crypto tokens via Policy Statement 15 December 2025 (following CP168 Oct 2025). Responsibility shifted to Authorised Persons to perform documented suitability assessments. Fiat-crypto tokens (stablecoins) remain DFSA-assessed. Privacy tokens and algorithmic stablecoins remain **Prohibited Tokens**. [arabianbusiness.com; nortonrosefulbright.com]

DFSA's Arabic alerts page (dfsa.ae/ar/alerts/byn-hol-lasol-lmshfr) confirms DFSA **does not regulate NFTs or utility tokens** in financial services unless they fall within the investment-token definition.

### 6.4 Live DIFC platforms

As of early 2026, **no DFSA-licensed fully-operational tokenized real-estate platform had publicly exited the sandbox** — the 96 sandbox EOIs suggest imminent pipeline. Prypco operates **Prypco Blocks** in DIFC for international non-resident investors as a complement to Prypco Mint (VARA).

## 7. Central Bank of UAE (مصرف الإمارات العربية المتحدة المركزي)

### 7.1 Payment Token Services Regulation (PTSR) — Regulation No. 2/2024

Issued **7 June 2024**, effective **6 July 2024**. Three regulated services: (1) Payment Token Issuance (إصدار رمز الدفع); (2) Payment Token Conversion (تحويل/تداول رمز الدفع); (3) Payment Token Custody & Transfer (حفظ وتحويل رمز الدفع). [rulebook.centralbank.ae]

Taxonomy:
- **Payment Token** = stablecoin (virtual asset maintaining stable value by reference to a fiat currency)
- **Dirham Payment Token (DPT)** — AED-pegged; requires CBUAE **full licence**; UAE incorporation mandatory; **may not pay interest/yield**
- **Foreign Payment Token (FPT)** — non-AED; foreign issuer may be **registered** with CBUAE; UAE use restricted to purchasing virtual assets/VA derivatives (Article 2(5))
- **Prohibited:** algorithmic stablecoins, privacy tokens, in-kind issuance

**Merchant acceptance (Article 2(7)):** UAE merchants may accept only a DPT for goods/services or an FPT for VA purchases. **"Within the UAE" excludes DIFC and ADGM**.

Reserves: 100% high-quality liquid assets; ≥50% cash in UAE banks; redemption at par within 1 business day; monthly external audits; daily reconciliation; segregated client wallets.

Transition period: 1 year ending **6 July 2025**. VASPs already licensed by VARA or SCA must obtain a **Non-Objection Registration (NOR)** from CBUAE to continue custody/transfer/conversion of stablecoins.

## 8. SCA → CMA (هيئة الأوراق المالية والسلع → هيئة سوق المال) — Federal Capital Markets

### 8.1 SCA replaced by CMA — 1 January 2026

**[Arabic-only intelligence — critical]** Federal Decree-Laws **32/2025 and 33/2025** transformed SCA into the **Capital Market Authority (CMA — هيئة سوق المال)**, effective 1 January 2026. CMA is SCA's universal legal successor. SCA-era decisions remain valid only insofar as not inconsistent with the new laws pending CMA implementing regulations. [Sources: sca.gov.ae/ar; zawya.com/ar; Cleary Gottlieb; Al Tamimi]

CMA framework (from Arabic Zawya): **"هيئة سوق المال تصدر إطار تنظيم الأصول الافتراضية، مؤسِّسةً نظاماً متكاملاً يتكوّن من خمس وحدات رئيسية وثمانية أنشطة خاضعة للتنظيم"** ("CMA issues virtual assets regulatory framework establishing an integrated system of five core units and eight regulated activities"). [zawya.com/ar]

### 8.2 Extraterritorial reach — Article 2(1)(d) FDL 33/2025

Covers **"any person targeting clients within the UAE, even if activity is conducted outside the UAE or from a financial free zone"**. Article 71 penalties: imprisonment ≥1 year + fines up to **AED 250 million**. Foreign issuers (including Luxembourg SPV) must submit documents, data, and financial reports to CMA when targeting UAE clients. DIFC and ADGM remain carved out.

**Direct Antweave implication:** A Luxembourg SPV issuing ERC-3643 tokens to UAE investors via Antweave's VARA Broker-Dealer triggers **CMA product-level registration** separate from VARA licensing. VARA licence auto-registers the VASP with CMA for nationwide reach, but **token admission to the CMA list is an independent gate**.

### 8.3 SCA–VARA Cooperation Agreement — 5 September 2024

**[Arabic-only intelligence]** The cooperation agreement was signed **5 September 2024** (not September 2022 as sometimes reported in English). Signatories Dr. Maryam Al Suwaidi (SCA CEO) and Matthew White (VARA CEO). Translated Arabic quote: **"We seek to build effective frameworks with partners to enhance the growth and stability of the virtual assets sector in the State, ensuring enforcement of legislation and AML combat"**. [sca.gov.ae/ar]

Operating rule: VASPs in/from Dubai (ex-DIFC) obtain VARA licence only and are auto-registered with SCA/CMA for wider UAE service; VASPs outside Dubai need direct SCA/CMA licensing.

### 8.4 SCA Decision 23/2020 successor regime

Chairman Decision 23/R.M/2020 on crypto assets (1 November 2020) was supplemented in Dubai by the 2023 SCA VA Exchange Regulation (Decision 26/RM/2023) and Rulebook Amendment (Decision 27/RM/2023). **Chairman's Resolution 15/RM/2025** (June 2025) created a dedicated regime for Security Tokens and Commodity Token Contracts — **RWAs expressly excluded from "security tokens" where the underlying is not itself a security**. Directly relevant: Antweave's real-estate ARVAs are not security tokens under this regime — they are ARVAs under VARA.

## 9. FTA (الهيئة الاتحادية للضرائب) — Corporate Tax & VAT Implications

### 9.1 UAE 9% Corporate Tax (Federal Decree-Law 47/2022 — المرسوم بقانون اتحادي 47 لسنة 2022)

Rate 9% on taxable income >AED 375,000; 0% below. **Free Zone Persons are taxable** but a **Qualifying Free Zone Person (QFZP — الشخص المؤهل من المنطقة الحرة)** retains 0% on Qualifying Income.

QFZP conditions (Article 18): Free Zone Person; adequate substance (CIGAs, assets, qualified employees, opex in the FZ); qualifying income; no standard-regime election; arm's-length TP; audited financials; non-qualifying revenue ≤ de minimis (lower of 5% or AED 5M).

### 9.2 Excluded Activities — Ministerial Decision 265/2023, updated by MD 229/2025

Article 3(1)(e): **"Ownership or exploitation of immovable property, other than Commercial Property located in a Free Zone where the transaction is conducted with a Free Zone Person"**. [Source: mof.gov.ae Arabic/English]

**Critical Antweave implication:** The DMCC entity holding AED 100M+ UAE real estate — if predominantly residential or non-FZ commercial — generates **Excluded Activity income taxed at 9%, not 0%**, even inside a QFZP. Only commercial property inside a Free Zone transacted with another Free Zone Person can be Qualifying Income. **Mainland Dubai rental/token income is fully taxable at 9%.**

**Tokenization service revenue** (platform fees, arranging) could qualify as QFZP income if conducted with Free Zone Persons or non-UAE customers. Tokenization revenue sourced from UAE **natural persons** (retail investors) is an Excluded Activity. If tokens represent **direct beneficial interest in UAE property**, income derives from immovable property and is Excluded regardless of where booked.

### 9.3 Transferring UAE property to a Luxembourg SPV — tax cost

**DLD Transfer Fee:** 4% of the higher of declared price or DLD valuation, on any change of legal owner including transfer to a foreign SPV. On AED 100M+ portfolio, baseline **AED 4M+** plus trustee and admin fees. [dubailand.gov.ae]

**Reduction paths:**
- **Gift transfer (0.125%):** only if transfer is from an individual to a wholly-owned entity of the same individual — **not applicable** to a DMCC-corporate → Luxembourg SPV transfer
- **Corporate restructuring route:** discretionary DLD reduction where no change in UBO, documented via resolutions, UBO affidavits, auditor letters — not automatic
- **DIFC SPV intermediary:** DIFC has a standing MoU with DLD enabling transfers to DIFC SPVs at **0.125% instead of 4%** where beneficial ownership is preserved. DMCC → DIFC SPV → Luxembourg SPV (as shareholder) is a viable step-down structure [10leaves.ae]

**Qualifying Group Relief (Art. 26)** and **Business Restructuring Relief (Art. 27)** defer gains on transfers between 75%+ commonly-owned UAE resident taxable persons — but a Luxembourg SPV is **non-resident** and therefore generally ineligible unless it has a UAE PE or elects resident status.

### 9.4 Media barter basis — the AED 100M question

FTA Barter Clarification **VATP042 (28 April 2025)** requires valuing non-monetary consideration at **fair market value on the date of supply**. For Antweave's historical **media barter (بارتر إعلاني / تبادل إعلاني)** acquisition:
- Tax basis of properties = **fair market value of advertising services rendered at acquisition**
- Contemporaneous FMV documentation (invoices, media rate cards, third-party benchmarks) is **critical**
- Under-documentation risks FTA recharacterising basis downward and inflating taxable gain at transfer/sale
- **Risk: FTA could impute AED 0 basis if documentation absent, taxing 100% of proceeds on transfer**

**Recommended action:** Commission an independent valuation report now for historical barter transactions to defend tax basis.

### 9.5 VAT on tokenization — the dual-classification problem

**FTA Public Clarification VATP040 (14 March 2025)** + Cabinet Decision 100/2024: transfer/exchange/custody (without fee) of **virtual assets** is **VAT-exempt retroactive to 1 January 2018**. [filings.ae]

**Dual-classification risk for tokenized real estate:**
- If tokens = **virtual assets** (financial services treatment): issuance/secondary trading VAT-exempt (domestic) or zero-rated (export of services to non-resident); input VAT recovery restricted
- If tokens = **direct real estate interest**: 5% VAT on commercial; exempt on residential (or 0% first supply within 3 years of completion)
- Platform fees = standard-rated 5%

**Recommended action:** File an **FTA Private Clarification** before primary issuance. ERC-3643 beneficial-interest tokens via a clean SPV wrapper are more defensible as financial instruments than direct property interests.

### 9.6 VAT on commercial property transfer to Luxembourg SPV

Commercial property transfer = 5% VATable supply (Special Payment Mechanism). Residential = exempt (or zero-rated first supply within 3 years). **Transfer of a Going Concern (TOGC)** treatment may be available if the whole rental business is transferred — but narrowly construed.

## 10. Shari'ah compliance for tokenized real estate

### 10.1 UAE Awqaf Fatwa No. 89043 (30 January 2018) — the foundational ruling

Arabic reasoning translated: *"Bitcoin is a digital currency that does not meet the legal and Shariah criteria (الضوابط الشرعية) that make it a currency subject to the rulings of dealing with official legal currencies recognised internationally. It also lacks the Shariah controls that make it a commodity (سلعة) capable of being exchanged for other commodities. Therefore, it is not permissible to deal with Bitcoin or other electronic currencies that do not meet the recognised Shariah and legal criteria…"*

**Critical opening clause:** *"…if a decision is made to regulate and adopt them and place them under a supervisory umbrella (مظلة رقابية) by those authorities, so that they meet the criteria that make them a legal currency…then the ruling on dealing with them would take the same ruling as dealing with officially recognised currencies."*

[Source: awqaf.gov.ae/en/Pages/FatwaDetail.aspx?did=88972, Arabic + English]

**Operational implication:** VARA-regulated, asset-referenced tokens backed by real estate — transacted in AED (as in Prypco Mint) — arguably **satisfy the "supervisory umbrella" proviso**. A Ras Al-Khaimah Primary Court (Case 87/2020) relied on Fatwa 89043 to void a OneCoin sale on **gharar** grounds, affirming binding civil weight.

The **UAE Council for Fatwa** (مجلس الإمارات للإفتاء الشرعي) has **not published** a distinct ruling on asset-referenced tokens or tokenized real estate — the posture is one of **tawaqquf (توقف — suspension of judgement) pending regulation**. [fatwauae.gov.ae]

The **International Islamic Fiqh Academy** (OIC, 24th session Dubai November 2019) similarly declined a definitive crypto ruling. [iifa-aifi.org]

### 10.2 AAOIFI (هيئة المحاسبة والمراجعة للمؤسسات المالية الإسلامية) standards

AAOIFI has 59 Shariah standards, **mandatory for UAE Islamic financial institutions since 2018**. Key references:
- **SS 17 (Sukuk)** and **SS 18 (Possession/Qabd)** — SS 18 §3/5 treats documents like bills of lading as **constructive possession (قبض حكمي)** — classical anchor for arguing a blockchain token in a wallet effects qabd of the underlying real estate
- **SS 57 — Gold Standard (2016, with World Gold Council)** — template for commodity-backed stablecoins; mandates spot settlement, allocated physical backing, real possession, zakat treatment
- **SS 59 — Sale of Debt (بيع الدين)** — restricts receivables trading; tokens representing pure murabaha receivables cannot trade at market value
- **Draft SS 62 — Sukuk (exposure 2023–2024)** — would require sukuk to be genuinely **asset-backed** (real title transfer to holders), not merely asset-referenced. Moody's (October 2024) warned this could slow issuance but **strongly reinforces the Shariah case for tokenized real estate that conveys real undivided ownership**
- **No dedicated AAOIFI digital-assets standard as of April 2026**

### 10.3 Gharar, Maysir, Riba analysis

- **Gharar (غرر — excessive uncertainty):** mitigated when (i) underlying property is specific/existing/identifiable; (ii) title clearly transferred to SPV/trust on behalf of holders; (iii) token rights (cashflow, redemption, governance) are explicit; (iv) issuer synchronizes with land registry. Dubai's DLD-issued Property Token Ownership Certificate synced on-chain with title deeds **directly addresses gharar**
- **Maysir (ميسر — gambling):** attaches to price-detached utility-less tokens. Tokenized real estate with rental yield and defined redemption is generally not maysir. Excessive leverage, short-selling, or derivative overlays re-introduce maysir risk. DLD's Phase 2 controlled secondary market constrains speculative distortion
- **Riba (ربا — interest):** avoided if returns derive from rental/usufruct (ijarah) or profit-and-loss sharing (musharakah/mudarabah) and financing uses murabaha/istisna, not interest-bearing loans

### 10.4 UAE Islamic banks — tokenization status

- **Dubai Islamic Bank (DIB / دبي الإسلامي):** Crypto.com partnership announced December 2024 to explore a Shariah-compliant platform; **no tokenized RE or on-chain sukuk launched as of April 2026**
- **Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank (ADIB / مصرف أبوظبي الإسلامي):** first Islamic bank on UAE KYC Blockchain Platform; **Smart Sukuk platform** via UAE regulatory sandbox — roadmap for tokenized sukuk but no live tokenized real estate [albayan.ae Arabic, emaratalyoum.com Arabic]
- **Emirates Islamic (الإمارات الإسلامي):** USD 750M sustainable sukuk May 2024; no tokenized offering [emiratesislamic.ae/ar]
- **Mashreq Al-Islami:** USD 500M debut sukuk on Nasdaq Dubai 2025; Head of Islamic Banking Ibrahim AlMheiri stated *"Asset tokenisation is increasing liquidity, allowing fractional ownership in real estate and Sukuks. Mashreq Al Islami is leading this transformation…"* — directional, not live
- **FAB (conventional reference):** USD 100M digital bond on HSBC Orion listed on ADX; CEO explicitly named tokenized sukuk and real estate-linked products as next asset class

**Net finding:** **No UAE-licensed Islamic bank has launched a publicly-disclosed Shariah-certified tokenized real estate product as of April 2026.** Prypco Mint is fintech-run with Zand (conventional) as banking partner; no Shariah certification announced.

### 10.5 Is a Shariah board required for a VARA Category 1 VA Issuer?

- **Regulatory answer: No.** VARA rulebooks contain no mandatory Shariah governance requirement; Shariah supervision sits with CBUAE's Higher Shariah Authority (HSA) for Islamic Financial Institutions only
- **Market expectation: Yes, if marketed as Shariah-compliant.** Any issuer targeting Islamic banks, takaful, or GCC sovereign/family offices must appoint either (a) an independent Shariah advisory firm (Dar Al Sharia — DIB's affiliate; Shariyah Review Bureau; Amanie); or (b) a dedicated Shariah Supervisory Board (هيئة الرقابة الشرعية) issuing a fatwa of compliance. Without it, HSA-mandated screening will reject allocation

### 10.6 Structural Shariah requirements (consolidated)

1. **Real undivided ownership (ملكية حقيقية مشاعة)** transferred to SPV/trust; not mere contractual reference
2. **Constructive possession (قبض حكمي)** via digital wallet record anchored in SS 18
3. **Tangibility threshold** — pool dominantly tangible/usufruct, not receivables
4. **No riba** — rentals/profit-sharing only
5. **Risk/reward alignment** — holders bear pro-rata property risk; no principal-protected "guaranteed" tokens
6. **Purpose screening** — underlying properties not materially used for haram activities
7. **Clear disclosure** — whitepaper, valuation, redemption, fatwa
8. **Takaful coverage** (not conventional insurance)
9. **Zakat treatment** per SS 57 analogues

## Conclusion

The Arabic-language record confirms Dubai has built a functional tokenized-real-estate market with VARA as the gateway, DLD as the property validator, and DMCC as the operational free zone — but reveals three dimensions that English-only research understates: (1) DLD's pricing-fairness gate as a separate regulatory step; (2) the transformation of SCA into CMA with explicit extraterritorial reach effective 1 January 2026; and (3) the conditional Shariah permissibility pathway opened by Awqaf Fatwa 89043's "supervisory umbrella" clause.

For Antweave, the path is workable but requires a layered structure (DMCC → DIFC SPV → Luxembourg SPV), contemporaneous FMV documentation to defend the AED 100M+ media-barter tax basis, parallel VARA and CMA registration, and — if targeting Islamic capital — a deliberate Shariah certification engagement with a UAE-recognized advisory. The Ctrl Alt partnership (white-label Category 1 issuance) combined with Antweave's own VARA Broker-Dealer licence is the most institutionally defensible configuration, with a direct DLD application in parallel to capture the Arabic-language "open door" signal that English coverage has underreported.

The regulatory window in 2026 — DFSA crypto-token reform (12 January), DLD Phase 2 secondary market (20 February), and ADGM FSRA amendments (March) — gives Antweave a genuine first-mover opportunity in a market projected to reach AED 60 billion by 2033, provided the structural and compliance groundwork is laid correctly now.
