Key Finding: Antweave is NOT like Prypco

Prypco is a platform/intermediary that facilitates investor-funded property acquisition from third-party sellers. Antweave, which owns its properties, is closer to a principal real estate sponsor using tokenization as a capital-markets wrapper. This creates a fundamentally different regulatory and risk profile under VARA's substance-over-form analysis.

2. Prypco Ownership Model

Prypco is a crowdfunding platform, NOT a property owner

Mint T&C Section 2.6 (verbatim)

"PRYPCO FZE will use the funds raised to acquire the subject Property on your behalf..."

Mint T&C Section 2.20 (verbatim)

"Seller means the individual or company that owns the legal Title Deed to the Property prior to the Investment Term."

This describes a classic crowdfunding/agency model:

  1. Prypco identifies a property owned by a third-party Seller
  2. Prypco launches an Investment Round to pool investor funds toward a Funding Target
  3. Once the Funding Target is met, Prypco uses those funds to acquire the property from the Seller
  4. The property is registered in investors' names (Mint) or in an SPV (Blocks)
  5. If the Funding Target is not met, funds are returned to investors

Revenue model: Acquisition fees + management fees + trading commissions. Known Sellers include Sobha Realty and Danube Properties.

3. Token Issuance Chain

Three distinct parties with clearly separated roles

Step 1: DLD
Legal Authority
Registers property title. Issues the legal right the token represents. Maintains the official registry as "source of truth."
Step 2: Ctrl Alt Solutions DMCC
Technical Minter
Operates DLD's tokenization engine. Mints tokens on blockchain via smart contracts. Provides custody (warm MPC omnibus account).
Step 3: Prypco FZE
Licensed Distributor
Markets and sells tokens. Operates secondary marketplace. Handles KYC/AML. Licensed by VARA as BD.

Important Nuance: "Issued by DLD" language

Mint T&C 3.1 states tokens are "issued by DLD and minted by DLD's tokenization engine, Ctrl Alt Solutions DMCC." This is technically imprecise. The DLD issues the property right (Title Deed); the token itself is created (minted) by Ctrl Alt. The T&Cs conflate the legal source of the property right with the technical creation of the token — phrased to give the token government imprimatur.

6. ARVA Framework

Asset-Referenced Virtual Asset — VARA's regulatory category

ARVA tokens derive their value from real-world reference assets. In the real estate context, they represent rights such as rental income distribution, voting rights, governance participation, and capital appreciation.

Prypco's Two-Token Model (announced November 2025)

  • Existing properties: Original title deed tokens coexist with newly issued ARVA tokens
  • New listings: Launch with both tokens from day one — a title deed token (legal ownership) and an ARVA token (VARA-regulated digital asset)

Implications for Antweave

  • Any token referencing Antweave's real estate would likely be classified as an ARVA
  • ARVA issuance requires Category 1 VA Issuance licence — currently only 2 entities hold this (Tokinvest + Ctrl Alt)
  • Antweave must partner with a licensed VA Issuer or obtain its own licence

7. Regulatory Implications for Antweave

VARA's substance-over-form principle

De Facto Issuer Risk

Even if a separate SPV formally issues tokens, VARA will ask who the real sponsor is. If Antweave owns the asset, selects it, markets it, controls disclosures, and receives proceeds — Antweave is the economic originator regardless of formal structure.

Related-Party Conflicts (Barter-Acquired Inventory)

  • True acquisition cost vs offering price — what is the spread?
  • Is property booked at FMV or barter value?
  • Are investors buying sponsor inventory at a premium?
  • Is there hidden monetization of non-cash consideration?
  • Who captures upside between acquisition and token sale?

What Antweave Must Do Differently

Role Separation

Separate asset-owning entity, SPV issuer, licensed BD/distributor, tokenization provider, property manager, and independent valuer/auditor.

Ring-Fence Each Property

One property, one clean title chain, one SPV, one valuation pack, one disclosure file, one waterfall, one investor rights package.

Over-Disclose Conflicts

Acquisition basis, related-party arrangements, consideration paid, valuation methodology, sponsor promote/equity spread.

Independent Valuation

Independent RICS-style valuation, legal title verification, independent disclosure review, fairness opinion if large spread.

Governance Framework

Investment committee, valuation committee, disclosure committee, conflicts policy, related-party transaction policy.

Don't Hide Behind SPV

Regulators look through to the real sponsor. Structure must be defensible on substance, not just paper.

9. Correction to First Email

What Was Said (Incorrect)

  • "Prypco sells tokens to/from their own inventory (principal dealer model)"
  • "Every trade is Prypco ↔ Investor, never Investor ↔ Investor"

What Is Actually True (Corrected)

  • Prypco does NOT hold or maintain an inventory of Virtual Assets (BD Disclosure 3)
  • The secondary market operates on a P2P basis — Investor ↔ Investor
  • Prypco acts as broker/arranger, providing the platform and earning commissions
  • Prypco does not repurchase tokens; no designated market maker exists

Corrected Formulation

  • Primary market: Prypco facilitates crowdfunded property acquisition. Investors pool funds to buy from a third-party Seller. Tokens issued by DLD / minted by Ctrl Alt.
  • Secondary market: P2P marketplace operated by Prypco as licensed BD. Investors trade with each other within a ±15% price band. Prypco earns commission but is not a counterparty.
