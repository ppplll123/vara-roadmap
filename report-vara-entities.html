Section 10 — Key Findings

  1. Only 2 entities hold VA Issuance licences — Tokinvest (Dec 2024) and Ctrl Alt (May 2025). This remains the most exclusive VARA licence category.
  2. Tokinvest's CEO Scott Thiel played a key role in shaping VARA regulations while at DLA Piper, providing the company with deep regulatory expertise.
  3. Binance's 25-month timeline from application to full licence is the longest documented case. Most entities take 12–18 months.
  4. Bybit has been in IPA status for 20+ months without progressing to a full licence, demonstrating that IPA does not guarantee final approval.
  5. Two IPAs have been withdrawn (WadzPay and MuseTech), confirming that failure during the licensing process is possible.
  6. Prypco has the strongest government backing for real estate tokenization, with an official Dubai Land Department partnership for legally recognized fractional property ownership.
  7. MANTRA's OM token crash (−90% in hours) did not result in licence revocation, indicating VARA separates market events from regulatory compliance assessments.
  8. One Central / DWTC is the dominant crypto hub — the vast majority of licensed VASPs are located there.
  9. Broker-Dealer is the most common licence type (78% of entities), serving as the standard entry-level VARA licence.
  10. The market is accelerating — 13 new licences were issued in H2 2025 and 8 already in Q1 2026.
